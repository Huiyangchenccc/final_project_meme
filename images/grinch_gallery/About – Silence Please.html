Silence Please is a speaker studio based in New York City.


We design high-fidelity sound systems that prioritize depth over volume, clarity over loudness, and presence over performance. Each piece is designed to reveal the quiet details in sound—the breath before a word, the touch of a string, the shape of a room.


Our space lives inside a former art gallery on the Bowery.

It’s part listening room, part tea house.

A place to slow down. To tune in and listen gently.


We believe silence is not the absence of sound,

but the presence of attention.


We started Silence Please to make something slower.

Something quieter.

Something that tunes us back to what’s always been there.


We don’t think of speakers as loud objects.

We see them as instruments of care.

Every detail—from acoustic tuning to physical form—is carefully considered to serve the listening experience.


We began with a single prototype in a small New York studio.

Then came a room.

Then a cup of tea.

Then others, drawn by the sound.


Silence, please.

For those who listen.
